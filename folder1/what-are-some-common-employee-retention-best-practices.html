What are some common employee retention best practices?

Employee retention is a constant challenge for many organizations. While some employees will leave for any number of reasons, others may be more difficult to retain because they’re not performing up to expectations or are actively looking for another job.In an effort to reduce turnover and increase productivity, many companies have adopted employee retention best practices. These practices include keeping communication lines open, providing constant feedback, and developing mentorship programs to help employees grow and develop new skills. Here is a list of some common retention practices organizations use to help retain their employees and reduce turnover rates.

Keep Communication Open

The first step in any retention program is keeping communication lines open. This means providing employees with regular feedback about their performance, how they’re progressing, and what they can do to improve.When employees feel like they are being kept in the loop, they are less likely to leave. And when employees feel like their managers are actively trying to retain them, they are more likely to stay put.Some common ways to keep communication open include holding regular one-on-one meetings, hosting regular company-wide meetings, and using online collaboration tools like video conferencing and online project management software.

Provide Constant Feedback

Another key component of any employee retention program is providing constant feedback to help employees grow, develop new skills, and stay engaged with the company.As part of an employee retention program, organizations may choose to implement an appraisal system where employees are regularly evaluated based on their performance.Employees who are given constant feedback are more likely to stay engaged with the company and less likely to leave. Constant feedback also helps managers identify potential problems in the workplace that they might otherwise miss. This can help managers proactively address issues before they become problematic. Some common ways to provide constant feedback include holding regular one-on-one meetings, hosting regular company-wide meetings, and using online collaboration tools like video conferencing and online project management software.

Develop Mentorship Programs

Mentorship programs help employees get the training and support they need to develop new skills and stay engaged with the company.Mentorship programs are especially important for younger employees who are just starting their careers. They can help employees with new job skills or career aspirations get the training they need to advance their career.Organizations that provide mentorship programs may choose to implement formal mentorship programs or informal mentorship activities such as one-on-one coaching sessions or mentorship groups. Formal mentorship programs may include training programs or online courses where mentors and mentees meet regularly to discuss progress and share advice. Informal mentorship activities may simply involve one mentor and one mentee meeting regularly to discuss progress and share advice.

Offer Training Opportunities

Another common retention practice is offering training opportunities.Organizations may choose to offer in-house training programs or partner with external trainers to provide specialized training.In-house training programs are especially useful for organizations that have a large number of employees who need specialized training.Organizations that partner with external trainers may choose to offer a variety of different types of training, including soft skills training, STEM training, and training in in-demand job skills.Organizations may also choose to offer online training programs where employees can take online courses to learn new skills.Organizations that offer training opportunities are more likely to retain their employees, since employees who are engaged and feel like they are being supported are less likely to leave.

Host Culture Events and Activities

Organizations may also choose to host culture events and activities to help employees connect with each other and the company.Hosting culture events and activities may be especially useful for organizations that have a large number of employees who don’t work together.Organizations that host culture events and activities may choose to host team-building activities, social events, or cultural celebrations.Hosting culture events and activities may also help organizations better retain employees who identify as members of underrepresented groups.Organizations that host culture events and activities may choose to host events and activities that celebrate diversity and highlight the benefits of diversity.

Hold Job Rotation Programs

Another common retention practice is holding job rotation programs.Organizations may choose to rotate employees through different departments or different roles within the same department.Rotating employees through different departments and roles may help employees gain a better understanding of the company and different areas of the business.Organizations that host job rotation programs may choose to host rotating employees on the management team so they can get a better understanding of how the business operates.Organizations that host job rotation programs may choose to host rotating employees on teams so they can get a better understanding of how different departments operate.Organizations that host job rotation programs may also choose to host rotating employees in different countries to help employees gain a better understanding of how different cultures operate.

Conclusion

Employee retention is a constant challenge for many organizations.
